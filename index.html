Life Expectancy vs GDP

Life Expectancy vs GDP in 2010

We dive deeper into the relation between Life Expectancy and GDP by observing the scatterplot and infer that with the per capita GDP increased in various countries, life expectancy increased across various countries and regions of the world in 2010. It looks like there is a correlation between these two elements, year by year GDP per capita grows and Life Expectancy increases. Clearly if you hover your mouse over the Regions in Africa, the countries there have a low life expectancy and low GDP. Viewer can draw his own inferences based on this scatterplot.

Interact with the Scatter Plot

Hover your mouse over the circles to view the country's life expectancy in years on y-axis and per capita GDP on x-axis. The size of the circles is proportional to GDP per capita to remark the difference in the graph. The Regions and countries within that region are shown in the legend. Hover your mouse over regions in the legend to view the countries in that region.

Parameters

Several parameters are included in the visualizations. In the bar-graph, there is a sort button that sorts the life expectancy across various regions in ascending and descending order. In the multiline graph as the user hovers his mouse over the line charts, he/she can see the year and the life expectancy across the four income groups. In the vornoi diagram as the user hovers the mouse over a circle, he/she can see the country, the per capita GDP and life expectancy for that country. The size of the circle represents the per capita GDP dollar amount for a country.

Triggers

There are several triggers that make the visualizations interactive and the user can play with the data by clicking on buttons, hovering over the line charts, bargraphs, vornoi diagrams. By selecting the values in the legends in the vornoi diagram, the viewer can filter the countries in that region and see the life expectancy of the countries of that region. By doing so the viewer can easily identify that the countries in African region have lower per capita GDP as well as lower life expectancy.

User interface events are listed, along with a description of how possible user events are communicated to the viewer.

About the visualization

The Narrative Visualization

Do wealthier countries live longer? - This narrative visualization follows an interactive slideshow and is built with scenes, annotations, parameters, and triggers. The scenes follow a uniform template for visual consistency and are ordered to best convey the message that there is some correlation between Income and Life Expectancy across years from 1960 to 2015. Guidance is provided on each slide and viewer is able to explore the data through the visualizations.

Scenes in the Visualization

The narrative starts with a powerful world image and a question whether wealthier countries live longer. Following this, there are bar-graphs, multi-line charts and vornoi diagrams that explore the correlation between life expectancy across regions, across income groups and across GDP of a country respectively. The layout and any visual structure in these scenes makes a viewer wonder the power of data visualization and explore further.

Annotations

The annotations follow a template for visual consistency and highlight specific data items or trends. At each visualization, there is a description indicating the annotations. The first scene has a bar-graph that shows the average life expectancy across regions of the world. After that each slide has annotations that guide the user to draw a conclusion and/or explore further in the data. All annotations are cleared on each slide and are independent of each other. All of them use the same font for visual consistency. When the user hovers over his mouse over the graphs, the tool-tips help navigate the data in the visualization.
